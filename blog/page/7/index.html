How Many Will Die to Bolster Abbas?

Jul 19th, 2007

Israel is about to release 250 jailed terrorists in an effort to bolster Palestinian leader Mahmoud Abbas. What the government of Prime Minister Ehud Olmert is really offering so that Abbas can prove he is capable of extracting concessions is an as-yet-undetermined number of Israeli lives. Israel’s Justice Ministry admitted on Wednesday that one-fifth of the terrorists Israel releases in these kind of deals and gestures immediately resume their efforts to kill Jewish men, women and children. According to figures provided by the Almagor Terror Victims Association, a good number of them have succeeded in doing so the second time around. At least 179 Israelis have been killed over the past seven years by Palestinians released from Israeli jails. Imagine if this had happened to the US. Imagine if Washington agreed to release a group of prisoners from Guantanamo, and one of them proceeded to strap on a bomb and kill Americans somewhere in the world. It would only happen once, because the US would never again even consider such a concession. And yet, Israel is pressured to repeat this scenario over and over again, with the government offering the typical justification that none of the 250 to be freed on Saturday have “blood on their hands,” meaning they have not participated directly in the murder of any Israelis. Of course, most of these prisoners tried to get blood on their hands, but were caught before they could do so. And the government is letting them go with the knowledge that a lot of them will again try to kill. In about six months we should know how many Israelis died so that Olmert could receive a pat on the back and a limited round of praise from the international community.

Where Is Europe When Real Refugees Are in Need?

Jul 19th, 2007

There is a major four-year-old refugee crisis resulting from the Arab cleansing of ethnic Africans from Sudan’s Darfur region, and I am wondering: Where are the offers of aid from European nations that were more than ready to give asylum to Palestinian killers? The poor, oppressed souls pouring out of Darfur are being forced to take up residence in ill-equipped tent camps, or to seek refuge in Egypt and Israel. But Egypt is not treating them much better than the Arabs of Sudan did, and Israel is unable to absorb any great number of these refugees. So why are the nations of Europe, who pretend to care so much about Palestinian refugees and other dispossessed peoples around the world, not opening their gates to the Sudanese? Why are they not offering to help? Tiny, resource-strapped Israel cannot offer these people a permanent solution, Egypt is mistreating them, and Arabs from neighboring Chad are now being invited to take over their former homes. These people have no where to go, and no one with the means to do so is helping them. What really infuriates me about this issue is that just five years ago, Europe provided asylum to 13 nun-abusing Palestinian terrorists after the Israeli army pinned them down in Bethlehem’s Church of the Nativity. Were those killers more worthy of being spared and aided than the men, women and children that have fled Darfur?

Did Bush Propose Summit to Save Olmert?

Jul 18th, 2007

Israel’s Labor Party is accusing US President George W. Bush of proposing a Middle East peace summit this fall in order to delay Prime Minister Ehud Olmert’s inevitable political demise. Party sources noted that Bush’s announcement of an international summit in his Middle East policy speech this week will blunt growing calls for Olmert’s removal. No one is willing to be responsible for political instability in Israel just before a major peace conference. This represents a major coup for Bush and his “two-state vision.” Despite mounting Israeli opposition to any further surrender of land, Olmert is the one viable national leader still dedicated to conducting significant withdrawals, which, of course, are essential to Bush’s desire to play midwife to “Palestine.” If elections are held in the coming months and Netanyahu becomes prime minister, the White House can forget about the uprooting of any more Jewish settlements any time soon, and certainly not before the end of Bush’s tenure as president. Even if Netanyahu loses the election to Ehud Barak, the Labor Party leader would definitely remember that he was crushed in the 2001 prime ministerial election precisely because he had offered so much to Yasser Arafat, an offer that most Israelis see as having encouraged the Palestinians to launch their “intifada.” And so, Bush needs Olmert to get the Jews out of Judea and Samaria if he is to have any chance of fulfilling his self-appointed task of bringing peace to the Holy Land.

Hamas Integral Part of Palestinians

Jul 18th, 2007

Italy’s foreign minister on Tuesday insisted that Hamas represents an integral part of the Palestinian Arabs, and must therefore be engaged rather than shunned. While I don’t agree that Hamas’ crimes should be overlooked and the group granted legitimacy, I do agree that it is a “significant and substantial” component of Palestinian Arab society, a “ground roots movement,” to use the words of Massimo D’Alema. And that in itself is very telling of Palestinian society. Hamas does not exist in a bubble, it is not some self-contained group of radicals completely disconnected from those around it. Hamas’ members - the thousands and thousands of gunmen and suicide bombers - are all recruited from among the Palestinian Arab public. There is obviously a very large pool of potential recruits for the group to fish in. Then there are the opinion polls that show widespread general public support for both Hamas as a movement, and its acts of violence against Israel’s Jews. And, of course, we have Hamas’ landslide electoral victory in last year’s parliamentary election, when a huge majority of Palestinian voters chose the terror group to lead them. D’Alema is right - Hamas is a significant and substantial part of Palestinian Arab society, a group that enjoys widespread support from a general public that also provides the group with a steady supply of fighters. The lesson that D’Alema and the rest of the international community fail to take from this is that a society that can give birth to and provide a firm power base for a group such as Hamas is not a society that is ready for peace with Israel.

IDF Lays Plans for Syria’s Defeat, but Will It Get to Use Them?

Jul 17th, 2007

A senior Israeli general on Monday confirmed that the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) are training for a full-scale war with Syria, and that plans have been laid for a swift victory. But in the event of a conflagration, will Israel’s leadership allow those plans to be implemented? According to Maj.-Gen. Eyal Ben-Reuven, who served as deputy commander of Israel’s northern forces during last summer’s war, the new battle plan calls for a rapid and overwhelming ground invasion of Syria. The reason the IDF wants as many boots on the ground in Syria as quickly as possible is because in a war scenario, Syria is expected to try to demoralize Israel by hitting its population centers with hundreds of ballistic missiles. Ben-Reuven noted that Syria itself would be unconcerned by mass military or civilian casualties, but would certainly try to play on Israel’s sensitivity to civilian losses. So the army has what sound like solid plans in place, and the troops are preparing to implement them should the need arise. The question is, does the political echelon have the guts to let the IDF do its thing if the predictions of war by a wide array of military experts prove accurate? My feeling is that Prime Minister Ehud Olmert is too afraid of a repeat of last summer’s failures to respond with any great force to low-level cross-border provocations by Syria. I also see Olmert as being way too controlling to let the IDF brass run the show their way. However, this time around Ehud Barak is in the picture, and he needs a big military victory in order to boost his chances of beating Likud leader Binyamin Netanyahu in future national elections. Barak can likely be expected to leverage his Labor Party’s participation in the government to force Olmert to give him free reign as defense minister. And, as I noted above, Barak needs a military victory, and so is probably ready to go to war. Barak is also a military man - in fact, he is Israel’s most decorated soldier ever - and as such will hopefully understand the wisdom behind the army’s plans.

Indoctrination of Death

Jul 17th, 2007

I have written on the issue of Palestinian children being indoctrinated to hate and fight Israel before, but the issue warrants rehashing in light of the new Hamas children’s program until recently hosted by a Mickey Mouse look-alike. To be sure, anti-Israel incitement in the Palestinian media and local school curriculums is nothing new and is ongoing. Unfortunately, that gross violation of the Palestinian Authority’s agreements with Israel is rarely covered by an international press more interested in blaming Israel for the Palestinians’ problems. Homas, however, partially remedied that problem when it committed copyright infringement against Disney, thereby making the indoctrination of Palestinian children headline news. The latest development in this saga was the “martyrdom” of Farfur the Mouse earlier this month at the hands of a man playing an Israeli security agent. In the clip, Farfur is seen trying to peacefully argue his right as a Palestinian to Jerusalem, which he accuses the Jews of conquering and stealing from his people. Farfur’s recently announced replacement, Nahool the Bee, is not prepared to be led meekly to slaughter like his “cousin,” and tells his young audience in the newest installment of _Tomorrow’s Pioneers_ that they must “take revenge upon the enemies of Allah, the murderers of the prophets [a common Islamic reference to the Jews]…until [Jerusalem] will be liberated from their filth.” As a father, watching these clips hit me hard. I often join my three-and-a-half-year-old son as he enjoys his favorite programs on Israel’s popular children’s channel. Completely innocent shows that make no reference whatsoever to the conflict around us. It literally brings tears to my eyes when I then think that children in my son’s same demographic in nearby Ramallah, Bethlehem, Hebron and elsewhere are being taught by their favorite fuzzy characters to hate and kill. It would devastate me if my son’s young mind was absorbing such lessons. This is an issue that needs to really be brought front and center. Israel, the US, Palestinian leader Mahmoud Abbas, and everyone else can negotiate and sign whatever pieces of paper they want, but as long as Palestinian children are being taught to hate and kill, there will be no peace. In fact, it is quite telling that official Palestinian media and the Palestinian Authority school curriculums are teaching children these things. It means they don’t see an end to the conflict in whatever “final status” agreement they expect to soon conclude with Israel. They want their future generations to be ready, mentally and physically, to fight the Jews.

No Human Rights for Palestinians

The international human rights organizations make a huge stink every time Israel commits alleged violations against the Palestinians, but are conspicuously mum when the Palestinians oppress, persecute, torture and murder one another. Ynet reported on Monday that a man arrested a week earlier by the Hamas overlords of Gaza was delivered dead to a local hospital with signs of torture and suffocation. A few days ago, I covered a story for Israel Today about a Palestinian suspected of aiding Israel’s war on terror having been tortured to death by Hamas. Sure, they make a few muted comments condemning the violence, but where are the white papers, the investigations, the special committees and emergency UN sessions these guys always employ when Israel is the target of their indignation? Once again, friends, the culprit here is anti-Semitism. Palestinian human rights are only worth defending when when doing so will help to further discredit Israel.

Trading Death for a Pat on the Back

Jul 16th, 2007

Yesterday I wrote on Prime Minister Olmert’s foolish and naive decision to offer amnesty to the worst Fatah terrorists in a ridiculous attempt to “bolster” the group’s leader, Mahmoud Abbas. Rather than foolish and naive, however, I think this decision can be better described as short-sighted and self-serving. It simply cannot be that Olmert and the rest of the Israeli and American leaderships are not aware that the terrorists who laid down their arms as part of the amnesty deal will pick them right back up when it serves their interests to do so. Did it somehow escape Olmert that these killers may have laid down one gun, but in doing so received enough cash to purchase a small arsenal of weapons? The real reason for these ludicrous decisions, as I see it, is that in Israel, like in America, the leadership is most concerned about political approval, about securing a positive “legacy.” A momentary pat on the back, a “thumbs up” from the White House seems to be more important to Olmert than ensuring the future security of his people. How else can one explain these moves by the Israeli government?

Olmert’s Foolish Gestures

Jul 15th, 2007

Prime Minister Ehud Olmert continues to best himself in making what are the most reckless, naive “goodwill gestures” to the Palestinians that I have ever seen. Today, Olmert confirmed that he will offer amnesty to wanted Palestinian terrorists belonging to Palestinian Authority Chairman Mahmoud Abbas’ Fatah movement. These people were at the top of Israel’s most wanted lists for a reason, but in the name of “peace” their brutal crimes against Jewish men, women and children are to be forgotten. Olmert justifies this move by noting the deal states that amnesty will be granted only to those terrorists who agree to disarm. Two problems: 1. Most of the Fatah terrorists are also members of the Palestinian Authority police forces, meaning they are legitimately armed as per Israel’s agreements with the Palestinians; 2. Who is going to enforce this disarming? Obviously not the Israeli army, since it will have promised to stop chasing these guys. Does Olmert believe that his good buddy Abbas will do the deed for him? Has Abbas or the Fatah ever given anyone any reason to believe they will honor any agreement? Olmert just seems to be on this political free-for-all as he scramble to salvage some kind of positive legacy for himself. Even though Ariel Sharon made many moves I disagreed with, at least he was more calculated and had some red lines (even if they did start to get a bit blurry towards the end). As one who believes in God’s Word and His plan for this nation, I am not worried about the ultimate outcome of everything. However, the number of people who die and the degree to which Israelis suffer along the way will be determined by the decisions of their leaders. May God quickly raise up new leaders for Israel who do not sacrifice the safety and well-being of their people in self-serving endeavors.

France Lends Legitimacy to Hizballah

Jul 15th, 2007

France has reportedly warned Israel to keep its hands of senior Hizballah officials invited to participate in a diplomatic conference in Paris. The warning came after French intelligence claimed it learned of a Mossad plot to nab as many of the Hizballah chiefs as possible. This is precisely the kind of thing that signals to Islamic terror groups that their use of brutal violence will ultimately carry no political consequences, and will likely further their own agenda. Just one year ago, Hizballah launched an unprovoked war of aggression from within Lebanese territory on a neighboring state. And that was after the UN had already outlawed Hizballah and demanded its removal as a fighting force. Now, like the PLO before it, Hizballah is being granted diplomatic protection by Western powers. You cannot outlaw a group and denounce its ways, then watch it attack a neighboring state, and then begin negotiating with it, all the while expecting that group to eventually moderate its views. The debacle of opening talks with Yasser Arafat and his PLO should be evidence of that, to say nothing of the appeasement that led to World War II. What Hizballah learned this weekend is that its acquisition of military power and its successful use of violence against Israel have gained it international legitimacy. Peace will not be made in such ways.
