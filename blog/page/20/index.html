Shameless Media Bias

Jul 25th, 2006

Most supporters of Israel readily recognize the underlying anti-Israel bias that taints the reporting of the mainstream international media (MSM). Forcing the MSM to acknowledge this phenomenon in an effort to change it, however, is a different story. Journalists from every other part of the world will vehemently deny practicing any bias or subjective reporting against Israel. Not so in the Arab world. Journalists employed by the top Arab media outlets - both television and newspapers - openly admit their disdain for the Jewish state, and their desire, in every interview conducted and report submitted, to make Israel look bad. > “The newscaster has to always be ready to make the Israeli interviewee uncomfortable, to pin him down in the narrow alleys of his lies,” said Mai al-Sharabani of the Al-Arabiya network. When the fast pace of events in the Middle East do not afford Arab television newscaster the opportunity to adequatly prepare the trap for their Israeli interviewees, they simply make use of “those precious final seconds of the interview, in which they can make a venomous remark that will not garner any response by the interviewee, for the simple reason that it is the end of the conversation,” notes _Ha’aretz’s_ Zvi Bar’el. But sometimes even that degree of control is not enough, and al-Sharabani admits to having to go to great lengths to restrain herself. > “There are times when I am forced to suppress a voice that rises within me, and which wants me to tell the Israeli interviewee: shut your mouth, you barefaced liar.” Al-Arabiya journalist Mohammed Abu Obeid concurs with his colleague regarding the importance of entering into an interview with Israelis under the presumption they are wrong: > “Newscasters understand the magnitude of the responsibility placed on them in interviewing Israelis. Millions of Arabs watch them, waiting to see how we will embarrass them or crush them in an interview.” While these examples will come as little surprise to anyone who is aware of the Arab world’s hatred for Israel, it does highlight the importance of choosing carefully who we allow to feed us information about the wider world. The Arabs are open about their violations of journalistic standards, but much of the MSM is guilty of the same.

What Is the ‘Root Cause’?

Jul 24th, 2006

There is much talk lately of what is the “root cause” of the current fighting in Gaza and southern Lebanon, with much of the world pointing an accusatory finger at Hamas and Hizb’allah. Former IDF chief Moshe Ya’alon took that one step further by pointing out that the Hamas and Hizb’allah aggression and provocations originated from areas Israel had completely vacated, disproving the long-held belief that “Israeli occupation” is the “root cause” of the wider Middle East conflict. Indeed, Arab aggression against the Jewish state predated Israeli control of Judea, Samaria, Gaza, southern Lebanon or the Sinai Peninsula. Ya’alon suggests the true root cause of today’s fighting is Iranian and Syrian support for radical Islamic elements bent on Israel’s destruction. He is mostly right. It is true that if Iran and Syria were not supporting and financing Hamas and Hizb’allah, the current crises might not exist. But the totalitarian regimes in Damascus and Tehran and the “radical” Islamists actually carrying out attacks on Israel are themselves not the root cause of the region’s troubles. The root cause, even deeper than the open belligerence of these characters, is the ongoing failure of probably 90-95 percent of the Middle East’s residents to accept the Jewish state at all. The core of the problem is education, and the Arabs’ refusal to teach the next generations to peacefully coexist with Israel. Millions of young Arabs are growing up believing Israel is a cancer that must be eradicated, and as long as this is the case, it won’t matter how many rogue regimes and terror groups you take out, more will always pop up.

Why the Fighting Must Continue

Jul 24th, 2006

Lebanese parliament speaker Nabih Beri Monday demonstrated precisely why Israel’s offensive against Hizb’allah must continue unabated for the time being. US Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice visited Beirut Monday to present a ceasefire deal that would see an immediate bilateral cessation of hostilities, followed by the insertion of an international force into southern Lebanon tasked with removing Hizb’allah arms from the area. Israel has already signalled its readiness to accept a new international force (preferably NATO-led) in Lebanon, and the withdrawal (if not total disarmament) of Hizb’allah north of the Litani River. But Beri, a Hizb’allah ally, rejected the deal outright, apparently preferring a continuation of hostilities to seeing his terrorist buddies de-fanged. I wonder if his Lebanese constituents would back that position? Beri did not close the door completely. He said the world could have its ceasefire, but on the aggressor’s terms: Israel halts its offensive immediately; Israel agrees to swap thousands of jailed terrorists for its two abducted soldiers; the world backs off while Lebanon alone decides whether or not a terrorist organization is allowed to rule its southern regions. Sounds like Israel needs to eliminate a few more bad guys before they get the message.

Civil Rights Group Gets Hot and Bothered

Jul 24th, 2006

The Association for Civil Rights (ACR) - your typical liberal everything-is-Israel’s-fault human rights organization - got all worked up Monday after IDF Chief of Staff Dan Halutz said every Hizb’allah missile barrage on Haifa would be met with a similar IDF bombing of southern Beirut. I thought the international community wanted Israel to react in a “proportionate” manner? What could be more proportionate than doing to your enemy exactly what he has done to you? The ACR claims that Halutz made his comments in an attempt to motivate Israeli forces on the basis of revenge. As if Israelis at this point need any further motivation to fight back against Hassan Nasrallah’s murderous hordes. His words would be better characterized as deterrence. The group also made the totally unsubstantiated claim that Halutz’s threat meant the “indiscriminate targeting of civilians and civilian interests.” But you see, that is where Israel’s response is no longer proportionate to Hizb’allah’s aggression. Unlike Hizb’allah, Israel takes as much care as possible to avoid harming the civilian population. That civilians are killed because Hizb’allah positions itself among them is not Israel’s fault, nor does it preclude Israel from targeting those positions.

Just One Problem, Kofi

Jul 21st, 2006

United Nations chief Kofi Annan, who consequently has a dismal record of conflict resolution, announced Thursday his plan for ending the fighting between Israel and Hizb’allah. An immediate and simultaneous cessation of hostilities by both sides, the transfer of Israel’s abducted soldiers to the Lebanese army, and the creation of yet another toothless international committee tasked with overseeing the disarmament of Hizb’allah - and everything should be right as rain. Just one problem: those terms are inconsistent with Hizb’allah’s present objectives and strategic goals. Annan’s naivety - or perhaps it’s a clever personification of gullibility in the employ of more sinister goals - has been long established. But even he can’t truly believe, or expect the Israelis to believe, that Hizb’allah is going to now just hand over the two Israelis it is holding and lay down its arms. Hizb’allah is in this fight for the long haul - the group has repeatedly stated and demonstrated that. Like its sponsors in Tehran and Damascus, Hizb’allah desires the annihilation of the Jewish state, and is not going to voluntarily fold for anyone; especially not the impotent UN and its soft-spoken leader. So, what happens when Hizb’allah fails to hand over the soldiers and allow itself to be disarmed? The answer is obvious for anyone that has been paying attention to this conflict. The UN committee will become bogged down in endless debate over what to do about Hizb’allah’s brazen defiance (remember Saddam?), two Israelis will live out the remainder of their days in traumatic captivity, and the Jewish state will suffer a crushing blow to its morale, having failed to defeat its tormenter or secure the freedom of its kidnapped soldiers. A clear recipe for further bloodshed in the near future. If Annan truly wants a resolution - and by resolution I mean a LASTING solution - to this particular facet of the wider conflict, he needs to let Israel finish off Hizb’allah.

World’s Broken Promises Led to Fighting

Jul 19th, 2006

The international community bears no small degree of responsibility for the current fighting between Israel and Hizb’allah. Israeli academic and sometimes government advisor Gerald Steinberg wrote this week about how in early 2000 he had participated in a meeting with European Union officials at the forefront of international pressure to get Israel out of southern Lebanon. > “In detailed talks that took place at the French ambassador’s residence in Jaffa…the Europeans assured us that once Israel retreated, Hezbollah would lose its raison d’etre as a ‘militia’ and transform itself into a political party. France and its partners would send peacekeepers to prevent terror and missile attacks against Israel, help the Lebanese army take control of the border, and disarm Hezbollah.” When the IDF withdrew a few months later, Europe and the rest of the world heralded Israel’s capitulation as a real chance for peace, and did absolutely nothing as Hizb’allah, drunk with victory, turned it into anything but. It took only five months for an emboldened Hizb’allah to launch its first cross-border raid, which resulted in the abduction and slaying of three IDF soldiers. Had Europe and the world fulfilled their promises to Israel back then, the region would not be in “crisis” today. Islam’s rejection of Israel may be the root cause of the violence, but the international community - in particular Europe and the UN - is its primary facilitator. If the world really wants to see peace and stability in the Middle East, it needs to learn from its mistakes, shut its mouth, and let Israel take care of business.

Hateful UN Ignores Israeli Humanitarian Crisis

Jul 18th, 2006

Displaying a hateful disregard for Israeli life, the United Nations Tuesday completely ignored the mounting humanitarian crisis in northern Israel and instead whined about Lebanese civilians displaced by a war started by their Hizb’allah brethren. While the UN claims some 500,000 Lebanese have been forced to flee their homes by Israel’s response to Hizb’allah aggression, some two million Israelis have either fled or are living in bomb shelters as a result of incessant terrorist missile fire. But acknowledging that fact would rob the UN of engaging in its favorite pastime - Israel-bashing. And check out this statement by UN Emergency Relief Coordinator Jan Egeland from Beirut: > “We hear and see a population which feels trapped as they are subject to indiscriminate attacks.” “Indiscriminate attacks”??? What about Hizb’allah’s relentless bombardment of northern Israel population centers with unguided weaponry? Isn’t that “indiscriminate”? As usual, the UN is trying to blur the lines and create moral equivalency between Israel its enemies. The fact is that Israel is very carefully picking its targets in an effort to minimize the loss of civilian life. Even when launching a simple retaliatory strike, an IDF artillery officer explained to Fox News Monday that Israel’s sophisticated equipment is able to pinpoint the origin of Hizb’allah rocket fire and return fire precisely on that location. If that location happens to be a residential home, apartment building or school, that is not Israel’s fault. That Hizb’allah has illegally chosen to situate its forces and strategic positions among the civilian population does not, under international law, restrict Israel from striking its enemy. The blood of any innocents killed is on Hizb’allah alone. This conflict is doing much to show UN anti-Israel bias for what it really is. Behold the new face of anti-Semitism.
