Palestinians Celebrate 9/11 Attacks on US

Sep 19th, 2007

While the Bush Administration pours millions of US taxpayers’ dollars into the Palestinian Authority in a vain hope that that regime will ever build and govern a viable state-like entity, the Palestinian Authority is busy celebrating the victories of America’s enemies. For those Americans who believe the Palestinian Authority of Mahmoud Abbas is either moderate or pro-American, take a look at this cartoon that featured prominently last week in a newspaper fully controlled by Abbas’ office: \ The same newspaper has carried similar illustrations ever since Al Qaeda killers downed the Twin Towers in New York City on September 11, 2001. That shouldn’t come as too much of a surprise, considering that the Palestinians erupted in mass street celebrations as the Twin Towers were burning and falling on top of thousands of Americans that fateful day. I remember watching television footage of Arabs just a few miles from me in eastern Jerusalem handing out candy and howling with joy over the fact that Osama bin Laden had slaughtered so many infidels. And that feeling of camaraderie with America’s enemies has clearly not waned. As recently as 2005, a public opinion poll showed that a firm 65 percent of Palestinian Arabs still supported Al Qaeda’s assault on the United States. That the Palestinian public proudly sees itself as an enemy of the US can be traced to its leadership, which continues to use all that US and European money to educate young Palestinians to hate not only Israel, but the entire Judeo-Christian West. Of course, that behavior can be traced back to Islam, which teaches all Arab Muslims, both the leaders and the general public, that all non-Muslim entities must ultimately be warred against and conquered. Should America really be spending its money in a way that helps forward such a message? Some will argue that cutting off the aid will wrongfully harm the Palestinian public for the mistakes of their leaders. But if the money being given to the Palestinians for education and other social assistance efforts is being used in such a way that teaches and trains future generations of Palestinians to hate and seek war, is it really helping them anyway?
