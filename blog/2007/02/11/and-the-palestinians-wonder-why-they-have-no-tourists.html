And the Palestinians Wonder Why They Have No Tourists

Feb 11th, 2007

A mob of Palestinian Arab youth violently attacked a tour bus carrying Canadian pilgrims at the Mount of Olives in Jerusalem on Saturday. The attack, which was perpetrated with stones and glass bottles, came amid Muslim riots related to very minor Israeli renovation work on an earthen ramp leading to the Temple Mount. And the Palestinians wonder why towns such as Bethlehem and Ramallah receive no tourists. It’s not because of Israeli roadblocks. It’s because the violent nature of the “Palestinians” - instilled in them by their elders from a very young age, sadly - makes those places extremely unsafe for visitors. Just think about it this way - the entire region (Israel, Judea, Samaria, etc) has for the past decade been embroiled in a pretty violent situation. And yet, foreigner tourists continue flocking to Israel. The Palestinian Authority-controlled territories, however, they avoid like the plague. That is because one population is prone to wild outbursts of violence, while the other is only trying to live in peace and security, employing violence and force only as necessary for its own protection. Regardless of what the newspapers print, visitors to the Holy Land are perfectly aware of who is the aggressor, and they are showing it with their feet.
