Following in Jimmy Carter's Footsteps

Feb 26th, 2007

PeaNUT-job Jimmy Carter claims his truest and most heartfelt desire is for Israel to live in peace, but all he really cares about is getting the Israelis and Arabs to sign another peace of worthless paper so he can cement his own legacy. Sadly, Carter’s is the same path being tread by President George W. Bush, as it was by Bill Clinton before him, and George Bush Sr. before him. Bush, with his messianic declarations about brining peace to the Middle East via his “two-state vision,” certainly finds himself agreeing with Carter’s assertion that “Israel must exchange Palestinian land [sic] for peace.” That America’s leaders failed to change their tune after Israel’s full surrender of the Gaza Strip not only did not result in peace, but actually increased the violence and led to Hamas’ electoral victory should be proof enough that their true goal is not peace, at least not for Israel. What concerns me more, however, is the failure of Israel’s friends among the American Christian community to unleash a hurricane of opposition to Bush’s plans in the wake of this realization. I know people here and there are writing to the White House, and there has been the occasional pro-Israel rally (all of which I feel lacked real fire and passion.) But why don’t Israel’s numerous friends in the US react to Bush’s policies regarding the Holy Land in the same impassioned manner that the liberal Left reacts to the war in Iraq? If Carter were still in office, I can imagine a far greater mobilization of Christian opposition to his policies vis-a-vis Israel. But Bush’s determined quest to play midwife to “Palestine” is no less dangerous. In my view, it is Bush’s strong stand against the America-hating Muslim “extremists” - which the president wrongly identifies as a mere fringe element in the Islamic world - that has kept the largely patriotic, America-loving Christian community from focusing too much attention on his “Road Map to Israel’s demise.” I don’t believe that Bush himself is evil, or has evil intentions. But like so many Christians in America, I believe he is blinded to God’s plans for Israel, and he is allowing his “war on terror” to be used by the true, underlying Enemy to blunt what could be massive American Christian support for and positive participation in that divine scheme. I remember that just after the September 11, 2001 attacks on the US an American friend told my colleague Stan Goodenough that America’s Evangelicals were still for Israel, but that we needed to back off on all the pro-Israel stuff for a bit and let them deal with the assault on their own nation. It is right to face down the demonic Islamofacist assault on America, but not at the expense of remembering that Israel takes center stage in God’s plans to defeat evil once and for all. If Americans are allowing themselves to be made ineffective in the battle to prevent the Enemy’s efforts to thwart God’s plans by dividing Israel, then their own war on terror is ultimately pointless.
