The Palestinians Are Clever PR Snakes

Feb 27th, 2007

Meryl Yourish already noted the diabolical cleverness of Hamas leader Khaled Mashaal when on Monday he turned the tables on Israel by insisting that Jerusalem “recognize the Palestinian state’s right to exist.” Mashaal remained on game today when he very vaguely hinted that maybe, just maybe, Hamas might think about making peace with Israel at some undefined point in the unforeseeable future. Said Mashaal after meeting with his good friends in the Russian regime: > “We have asked the international community to take rapid steps to lift the embargo imposed on the Palestinian people [sic] … This will create a political climate that could open a political prospect in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.” Naturally, the mainstream media was quick to push out stories about how Hamas was finally on the road to “moderation.” Mashaal had shrewdly given the international community just enough for those nations still focused on extracting “Palestinian” compliance with basic peace principles to get back to what they love doing best - dissecting biblical Israel. Never mind that Mashaal qualified his above remarks by stating that future “peace” must be based on “Palestinian conditions,” which include such things as the “right” to flood Israel with millions of so-called “Palestinian refugees” - conditions that Israel and Mashaal alike know are meant to bring about the demise of the Jewish state. That doesn’t matter to the main power brokers behind the “peace” process, who keep pushing such issues off until the point of final status talks, at which point Israel will be strong-armed into accepting the “Palestinian” conditions or risk being accused of bogging down negotiations just as “peace” was in sight. All that matters now is that Hamas said it wants peace! Right?
