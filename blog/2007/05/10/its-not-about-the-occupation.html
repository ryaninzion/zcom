It's Not About the 'Occupation'

May 10th, 2007

In an interview with _The Jerusalem Post_ this week, British Ambassador to Israel Tom Phillips contended that Israel’s negative image in Britain and the world stems from it being an “occupying power.” He couldn’t be more wrong. Phillips, who tried to strike a conciliatory note toward Israel in the interview, appears to suffer from the same shortness of memory that plagues most Israeli and Western officials caught up in trying to solve the Israeli-Arab conflict. When Phillips and other Western diplomats speak of the “occupation” today, they are referring to Israel’s control of Judea, Samaria and the Gaza Strip, areas which were liberated by Israel in 1967. But even before 1967, Israel was regularly demonized by the mainstream international press and the Arab world because of its very existence. There was no “occupation” in the way the international community speaks of it today, but still Israel was criticized as an “occupying power.” So, Mr. Phillips is wrong. Even if Israel surrenders every piece of land the so-called Arab “moderates” are demanding today, the critical press Israel’s receives will not cease. The Arabs, even the “moderates,” have made it perfectly clear that they eventually want all of the land today ruled by the Jews. The problem for them is Israel itself. And the international community and its press have an unfortunate tendency of unquestionably flowing with the changing dictates of the Arab world.
