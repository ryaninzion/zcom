More War on Terror Double Standards

May 27th, 2007

Lebanon’s military forces have for the past week been pounding Islamic terrorists operating out of a Palestinian “refugee camp” in the north of the country. Not wanting to risk large military casualties, the Lebanese army has primarily been using tank fire and artillery to target their enemies. According to numbers presented by the media, at least 50 terrorists have been killed, along with untold numbers of civilians. All of the above is fine and well. I certainly feel regret that civilians unable to defend themselves are being caught up in this, but the Lebanese absolutely have the right to make war upon their terrorist foes, even if that enemy is using a civilian population as a shield. And the Lebanese have the right to do so in a way that will most minimize casualties to their own forces. What is not fine and well is that the international community is so supportive of the Lebanese anti-terror offensive, while being so vocally opposed to every Israeli effort to extinguish the scourge of Palestinian terror. In an editorial posted to its website on Saturday, the _Jerusalem Post_ drew a parallel between the ongoing Lebanese operation and Israel’s Operation Defensive Shield, a massive April 2002 assault on terrorist positions throughout Samaria. The scope of both operations is similar, from a military viewpoint. The international reaction, however, could not be more different. During the Israeli operation, the bloodiest fighting of which took place in Jenin, the Arab world was generally supported by the international media and UN in its claims that Israeli forces were committing a massacre of Palestinian civilians. Even after the dust settled and it was clear no massacre had occurred, UN bodies and human rights groups still conducted investigations ending in the predictable accusation that Israel had committed war crimes. All of this despite the fact that Israel avoided the use of air power and artillery in order to minimize not its own casualties, but those of the Palestinians. Meanwhile, the Lebanese are pounding the Palestinian suburb of Nahr el-Bared with hundreds of artillery shells every day, killing who knows how many people and wreaking untold destruction. And where are the Arab League, the human rights groups and the international media? Mum. Everyone of them is mum. No calls for an investigation, no wild claims of a massacre, no pleas for the protection of innocent Palestinians. This, my friends, is ample evidence that the Arab world and the international community do not really care about the so-called “Palestinians.” What they care about is eradicating the Jewish state, and the Palestinian Arabs just happen to be the best tool to accomplish that at present.
