If Jerusalem, Then Also the 'West Bank'

May 14th, 2007

Apparently, Israeli Foreign Minister Tzipi Livni is firmly opposed to dividing Jerusalem and surrendering its eastern half to the Arabs, while unblinking she advocates the division of the Land of Israel and the surrender of her nation’s biblical heartland. Either Livni is just following the polls, which is likely considering her chance to steal the premiership from Olmert, or she is truly deluded. But even if she is just following the polls, that means the majority of Israelis who voted in those surveys are themselves deluded. You see, Israel cannot insist that it has every right to maintain control over a united Jerusalem, while acquiescing to demands that it relinquish all claims to Jude and Samaria. If Jerusalem biblically and historically belongs to Israel and the Jewish people, then so do Judea and Samaria. To relinquish the claim to one puts the claim to the other on very shaky ground. And that is precisely how the Arabs will view Livni’s recent comments. If Israel’s leaders are so ready to part with Judea and Samaria, the Arabs must be full of hope that sooner rather than later the Jews will be likewise convinced to give up their holy city as well. It is the height of foolishness for Israelis to believe that they can firmly and unquestionably hold on to some parts of this land (like Tel Aviv), and then turn around and agree with the Arabs that other parts are under “illegal Israeli occupation.” The Arabs do not see the land in the same divisions as most Israelis. They claim it all. Either all of its is occupied or none of its is occupied. For the Arabs, it is a black and white issue, and the Israelis would do well to remove their tinted sunglasses so they can get a clear view of the situation.
