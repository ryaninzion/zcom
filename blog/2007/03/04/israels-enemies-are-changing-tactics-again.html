Israel's Enemies Are Changing Tactics Again

Mar 4th, 2007

For decades, Israel’s enemies made no pretense of desiring peace with the Jewish state, and instead attempted at every opportunity to destroy the nation militarily. When it became clear that that was not going to happen, at least not under present circumstances, the Arab nations turned to diplomacy, albeit in a relatively hostile manner, to achieve their aims. While those hostile diplomatic efforts continue, it appears some of Israel’s Arab enemies are beginning to realize that they have a better chance of success if they offer more carrot and less stick. And so we find ourselves in a situation today where many officials in Jerusalem are getting all excited about the prospect of the Arab League, meeting later this month in Riyadh, removing the one really disagreeable clause from an otherwise acceptable Saudi peace proposal first presented in 2002. That proposal offers Israel peace and normal relations with the entire Arab world in return for a full surrender of the Golan Heights, Judea, Samaria and eastern Jerusalem. Now, while Israeli politicians are wary of offering to give up all that land, most are sadly willing to part with a great deal of their biblical patrimony for the promise of “peace.” The only part of the Saudi proposal that even the current defeatist government was unable to swallow was the demand that Israel allow millions of so-called “Palestinian refugees” to take up residence inside the borders of sovereign Israel - which would of course spell the end of the Jewish state. With the Saudis and others increasingly trying to sweet-talk Israel into signing a proverbial “deal with the devil,” it is not unfathomable that the Arab League will agree to at least downgrade the whole “Palestinian right of return” demand. And if that happens, many will ask, “So, why not agree to the deal if Israel is for the most part getting everything it ever asked for?” From a human perspective, there is one very good reason to still reject the proposal even if the “Palestinian refugees” are completely written out of it, and that reason is the volatility of the Middle East. Israel could surrender its most strategically important regions (the Golan, Jordan Valley and central hill region) in return for peace with the current Arab regimes, but in this neighborhood, you never know who will be in power tomorrow. There is no guarantee that 5, 10 or 20 years down the road, violently anti-Israel dictators just crazy enough to go to war will not be in power in Syria, Jordan, Egypt and Saudi Arabia. From a biblically-minded perspective, the aim of the true Enemy is, of course, to reverse the God-ordained restoration of Israel and thereby attempt to prevent the final fulfillment of the divine plan of global redemption.
