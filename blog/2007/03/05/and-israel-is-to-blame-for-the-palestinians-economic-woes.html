And Israel Is to Blame for the Palestinians' Economic Woes?

Mar 5th, 2007

Amidst the din of weeks of “Palestinian” infighting, the media can be excused for missing the story that since December 1, Muslim terrorists have bombed 45 Internet cafes in the Gaza Strip. Forty-five! In three months! Imagine if that was happening in the US. It would be declared a terror epidemic. But alas, this is only the latest example of the Palestinian Arabs’ propensity for economic self-destruction. Remember after the Gaza “disengagement” of 2005, when Israel left millions of dollars worth of advanced farming equipment and greenhouses behind for the “Palestinians” to use? And remember how the “Palestinians” promptly destroyed everything? Thousands of Gazan Arabs are today living on pennies because their own people are blowing up their family businesses and burning down generous agricultural gifts from Israel and the the international community. And still, everyone insists that it’s Israeli checkpoints that are the only barrier to an explosion of growth in the “Palestinian” economy. Right. If the peace activists and foreign diplomats really want to better the lives of the Palestinian Arabs, maybe they should focus less attention on criticizing Israel and more on convincing the “Palestinians” to actually behave like a civilized and productive society. If they did, there would be no need for the Israeli checkpoints.
