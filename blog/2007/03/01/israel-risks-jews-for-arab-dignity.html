Israel Risks Jews for Arab 'Dignity'

Mar 1st, 2007

Israel on Wednesday went one step further in making good on its promise to, yet again, sacrifice its own security for the sake of Arab “dignity” by easing airport security checks on the one group of people capable of mass-murdering airline passengers: Arab Muslims. In response to the nagging of Israeli Arab lawmakers over the “degradation” of Arab passengers at Israel’s airports, the head of the Israel Security Agency (Shin Bet) promised to spend huge amounts of money to install new technology and ensure that all passengers are checked the same. But if the Arabs really want these kinds of “humiliating” checks to stop, maybe they should focus on getting the more violent elements of their society to shape up. You see, the real problem is that most of the Arabs (**and I say this knowing that there are many truly good, loyal Israeli Arabs**) don’t see the fact that some of their number engage in anti-Israel violence as a problem. In fact, they celebrate violence against Israel by their own and by neighboring Arab entities. (Read more about Israeli Arab support for Hezbollah rocket attacks on Israel last summer.) And still they have the audacity to demand that Israel change its ways, that Israel bend over backwards and put itself at risk to make them feel like equal citizens when they applaud the enemies of the state? This whole whining and moaning bit seems to work really well for the Arabs, not only here in Israel, but also in America and Europe. Maybe its time that we non-Arabs start to whine and moan every time the Arabs do something we don’t like and demand they change their behavior and rein in their bad apples. And before anyone lays into me about how I can’t possibly know what these poor Arab victims are going through, I want to note that years ago, before I was married and had a family of my own, I was regularly targeted as a single male passenger for extra security checks at Israel’s international airport. While one time I did get rather annoyed at having missed my flight, I would hardly describe the experience as “humiliating,” nor would I demand the Israelis put their lives at greater risk for the sake of my own pride.
