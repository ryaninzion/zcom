Trading Death for a Pat on the Back

Jul 16th, 2007

Yesterday I wrote on Prime Minister Olmert’s foolish and naive decision to offer amnesty to the worst Fatah terrorists in a ridiculous attempt to “bolster” the group’s leader, Mahmoud Abbas. Rather than foolish and naive, however, I think this decision can be better described as short-sighted and self-serving. It simply cannot be that Olmert and the rest of the Israeli and American leaderships are not aware that the terrorists who laid down their arms as part of the amnesty deal will pick them right back up when it serves their interests to do so. They must know that the terrorists were more enticed by the $15,000 Abbas offered for every M-16 assault rifle than by the prospect of peace and reconciliation with the Jewish state. Did it somehow escape Olmert that these killers may have laid down one gun, but in doing so received enough cash to purchase a small arsenal of weapons? The real reason for these ludicrous decisions, as I see it, is that in Israel, like in America, the leadership is most concerned about political approval, about securing a positive “legacy.” A momentary pat on the back, a “thumbs up” from the White House seems to be more important to Olmert than ensuring the future security of his people. How else can one explain these moves by the Israeli government?
