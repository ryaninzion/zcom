The Palestinians Don't Want Compromise

Jul 25th, 2007

Israeli prime ministers are able to increase their public backing by concluding even minor compromise agreements with the Arabs, but the mainstream media and world leaders are 100% mistaken in asserting that such deals provide long-term political traction for Palestinian leaders. This misguided line of thinking featured once again in a _Ha’aretz_ story published on Wednesday regarding Prime Minister Ehud Olmert’s offer to negotiate an “Agreement of Principles” toward the establishment of a Palestinian state with PLO leader Mahmoud Abbas. The leftist newspaper maintained that such an agreement, which Israel hopes and assumes would be based on mutual compromise, would “offer both Abbas and Olmert domestic political gains, and the Palestinian leader will be able to use it as part of his reelection campaign.” It is hard to understand why after more than a decade of the “peace” process these guys still don’t get it. Abbas cannot go back to the Palestinian public without 100% of their demands being met. It is what he has been promising them for years, and it is what the Palestinian education system is teaching Palestinian children must be extracted from the “criminal and blood-thirsty Jews” one way or another. The Palestinians have been conditioned by their leadership to accept no compromise, so you can be sure that same leadership will never conclude the peace process without having all of its demands met in full. That is precisely the reason Yasser Arafat turned down Ehud Barak’s final status deal at Camp David in 2000. Arafat knew that the little bit of compromise Barak was asking him to make would ultimately be unacceptable to the Palestinians, and definitely would not help him in retaining his dictatorial powers. Going down this path of folly, it seems the best Israel can hope for is a bilateral agreement that does include some degree of Palestinian compromise over the land, but which will without doubt justify in the minds of the Palestinians a continuation of the “resistance.” Israel will get its piece of paper, and the Palestinians will get most of “their” land, but the attacks will continue. Abbas will claim he is at peace with Israel, but that he simply cannot control or contain the Hamas rogue in his midst. Arafat permitted the establishment of Hamas for just such a purpose - so that he could pretend to be engaged in meaningful peace talks with Israel, while anti-Jewish Palestinian terrorism continued unabated. This is what Israel’s current leadership and the international forces influencing it are offering Israelis.
