The Truth About Mohammed

Aug 26th, 2007

I just finished reading _The Truth About Muhammad: Founder of the World’s Most Intolerant Religion_ by Robert Spencer, and want to encourage anyone interested in a deeper examination of why Muslims are today at the center of the vast majority of conflicts around the world to pick up a copy. Spencer notes that, just as Jesus is the ultimate model for Christians, Mohammed represents for Muslims the perfect example of how a servant of Allah is to conduct his or herself. As such, in order to gain a better understanding of why modern Islamic claims to be a “religion of peace” ring so hollow, one must closely examine the life of Mohammed himself, as taught to Muslims through the Koran and Hadith. While Jesus sought to reach people through love and compassion, individuals and tribes that refused to bow to Allah and Mohammed paid and continue to pay a gruesome price. Mohammed was first and foremost a war chief, and a close look at his life reveals that his primary tool for the spread of Islam was violence. It is, therefore, little wonder that violence continues to be such a defining characteristic of Islamic societies today. Spencer also goes into why Islam is no friend of Christians and Jews, despite efforts to paint adherents the three faiths as servants of the same deity. Muslim groups in the West are fond of quoting the handful of Koranic passages that appear conciliatory toward Christians and Jews, while ignoring the far larger number of passages that command Muslims to subjugate, expel and destroy those who do not accept Allah and his messenger. There is a lot more in the book than what I have touched on above, and every chapter is meticulously researched. _The Truth About Mohammed_ is also a great index of all those early Islamic texts that give the lie to present day pronouncements about Islam that don’t seem to jibe with the actions of Muslims around the world.
