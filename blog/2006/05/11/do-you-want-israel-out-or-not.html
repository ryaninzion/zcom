Do You Want Israel Out or Not?

May 11th, 2006

The international community’s reaction to Israel’s intention to hand most of Judea and Samaria back to the Arabs without asking anything in return seems to suggest the world doesn’t actually want the “Israeli occupation” to come to an end. For decades the international community clammored for Israel to relinquish its biblical heartland. Eventually, under tremendous pressure, Israel acquiesced and entered into land-for-peace negotiations with the so-called “Palestinians.” After more than a decade of “Palestinian” intransingence, culminating in the overwhelming electoral victory of Hamas, a group openly and unswervingly dedicated to Israel’s destruction, Jerusalem has decided there simply is no viable partner with whom to conclude an agreement. But did Israel respond - as it should have - by saying, “Look, we told you so, the Arabs don’t want peace. Now let us be to administer these liberated lands as only we can”? No, Israel’s response is to throw up its hands and just give to the Arabs almost all of what they are demanding. And now the world wants Israel to hold its horses and _not_ surrender the bulk of the land to the Arabs? Do they want Israel out or not? I guess the problem for the world is that by unilaterally surrendering, Israel will be more free to set the terms of its own surrender. If it were done at the negotiating table, the world could pressure Israel to meet 100% of Arab demands, rather than just 90%. One hundred years ago, the Jews were certain that if they could only reestablish their own state they could finally be masters of their own destiny, able to make decisions they felt were best for their own people regardless of the whims of the nations. The international community today is determined to make sure this is not the case.
