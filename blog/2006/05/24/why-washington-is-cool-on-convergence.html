Why Washington Is Cool on 'Convergence'

May 24th, 2006

Many have expressed surprise over the fact that while Israel is preparing to surrender nearly all the lands demanded by the Arabs, Washington - the primary driver of the land-for-peace process - maintains a cool and distant approach to the Israeli initiative. Speaking to Arutz 7, I believe Yoram Ettinger, a former official at the Israeli Embassy in Washington, hits the nail on the head: > “The State Department has always been in favor of Israel’s return all the way to the 1949 borders…” and “there are those in the State Department who feel that [Olmert’s convergence] would be sufficient and would likely lead to a return to the ‘49 borders - but others see how Israel is planning to annex settlement blocs, and cannot agree.” The United States government, and in particular the State Department, are less than enthusiastic about the planned withdrawal because, at least initially, Israel, and not the Arabs, will decided how much land is relinquished.
