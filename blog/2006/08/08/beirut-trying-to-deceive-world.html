Beirut Trying to Deceive World

Aug 8th, 2006

The Lebanese government, in what news agencies described as an “extraordinary” event, decided unanimously Monday to deploy up to 15,000 troops in southern Lebanon, provided Israel withdraw its forces first. That decision is also certain to cause excitement in Washington and the capitals of Europe, as well as the halls of the UN. But weren’t all of these world leaders telling us just a few weeks ago that the Lebanese army was too weak to confront Hizb’allah, and therefore could not be held directly accountable for the terrorist aggression that sparked the current crisis? Is that still true, or has the Lebanese military suddenly become a powerful and capable fighting force? If it is the former, then who cares if Lebanon sends its army south at this point. Without a force capable of removing the Hizb’allah threat to northern Israel, this whole war is just going to repeat itself a few months or years down the road. In fact, Beirut is attempting to deceive the international community, even as Lebanon’s political and military leaderships make no secret of their support and admiration for Hizb’allah. Immediately following Monday’s cabinet decision, Lebanese Information Minister Ghazi Aridi refused to answer in the affirmative a reporter’s question on whether the troops being sent south would be tasked with disarming and removing Hizb’allah from the border regions. That should have sent up red flags right away. Shortly after, a senior government source indicated to Reuters that Beirut had taken the decision with the sole intention of forcing a revision of the US-France draft ceasefire proposal, which it sees as being too in line with Israel’s position on how to end the fighting. On one point there should be no confusion: If Lebanon deploys its army to the south, it will *not* prevent Hizb’allah attacks on Israel by forces of arms. Nor will Hizb’allah see the deployment as a true deterrent to its aggression against the Jewish state. All this move will accomplish is to provide Hizb’allah with additional human shields, and to deter future Israeli defensive operations for fear of having to engage its neighbor’s official armed forces and thereby sparking a wider regional conflict. Remarks by the head of Lebanon’s military and statements on the army’s official website to the effect that Hizb’allah is a *strategic national asset* would suggest that is precisely Beirut’s aim.
