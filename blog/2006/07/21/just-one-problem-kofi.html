Just One Problem, Kofi

Jul 21st, 2006

United Nations chief Kofi Annan, who consequently has a dismal record of conflict resolution, announced Thursday his plan for ending the fighting between Israel and Hizb’allah. An immediate and simultaneous cessation of hostilities by both sides, the transfer of Israel’s abducted soldiers to the Lebanese army, and the creation of yet another toothless international committee tasked with overseeing the disarmament of Hizb’allah - and everything should be right as rain. Just one problem: those terms are inconsistent with Hizb’allah’s present objectives and strategic goals. Annan’s naivety - or perhaps it’s a clever personification of gullibility in the employ of more sinister goals - has been long established. But even he can’t truly believe, or expect the Israelis to believe, that Hizb’allah is going to now just hand over the two Israelis it is holding and lay down its arms. Hizb’allah is in this fight for the long haul - the group has repeatedly stated and demonstrated that. Like its sponsors in Tehran and Damascus, Hizb’allah desires the annihilation of the Jewish state, and is not going to voluntarily fold for anyone; especially not the impotent UN and its soft-spoken leader. So, what happens when Hizb’allah fails to hand over the soldiers and allow itself to be disarmed? The answer is obvious for anyone that has been paying attention to this conflict. The UN committee will become bogged down in endless debate over what to do about Hizb’allah’s brazen defiance (remember Saddam?), two Israelis will live out the remainder of their days in traumatic captivity, and the Jewish state will suffer a crushing blow to its morale, having failed to defeat its tormenter or secure the freedom of its kidnapped soldiers. A clear recipe for further bloodshed in the near future. If Annan truly wants a resolution - and by resolution I mean a LASTING solution - to this particular facet of the wider conflict, he needs to let Israel finish off Hizb’allah.
