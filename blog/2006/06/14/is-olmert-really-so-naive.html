Is Olmert Really So Naive?

Jun 14th, 2006

Do Israel’s leaders - and for that matter, American and European officials - really believe that PLO chief Mahmoud Abbas is an opponent of anti-Jewish terrorism? They sure do say so often enough, though Abbas himself repeatedly contradicts that notion.In the latest example, former US State Department official Aaron Miller noted that Abbas’ endorsement of the “prisoners’ peace plan” is in fact an official sanctioning of terrorist violence against Israeli Jews. Certain clauses of the much-reported document call for the establishment of a “Palestinian” body responsible for coordinating all attacks on those Jews who continue to live in Judea and Samaria. It openly condones the continued use of terrorism against men, women and children until “Palestinian” demands are fully met. World leader and the international press, of course, only focused on those parts of the document that signaled a vague _de facto_ recognition of Israel’s sovereignty. Nor is this the first time Abbas has publicly endorsed “Palestinian” terrorists and their actions. During his election campaign, Abbas made seea point of appearing at public rallies with popular terror chiefs and referring to them as his “brothers.” Last summer, Abbas played to popular sentiment by describing Israel’s withdrawal from Gaza as a victory for the “resistance.” It does not matter what Abbas says to the international press or world leaders. These are the things he is telling his own people, and these are the things that will influence their actions. Israel and the world finally woke up to the fact that Yasser Arafat was not a good guy. It is time to likewise recognize Arafat’s long-time comrade-in-arms, Mahmoud Abbas, for what he really is.
