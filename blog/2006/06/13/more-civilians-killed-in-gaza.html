More Civilians Killed in Gaza

Jun 13th, 2006

This time they were in fact killed by Israeli munitions. But their blood is still on the hands of the terrorists who attempted to use the civilians as cover.With more than 100 rockets and mortars fired at Israeli communities since the weekend, the IDF is keen to prevent any further launches before a catastrophe ensues. A car carrying three Islamic Jihad terrorists AND the Katyusha rockets they were about to fire would, therefore, make an obvious target - one that rational people would do everything to steer clear of. And yet, after the first Israeli missile obviously failed to kill the Jihad rocket crew, “Palestinian” civilian bystanders rushed to the damaged vehicle in an effort to save their venerated terrorist brethren. Is it just me, or is the natural reaction not to move away from bad guys being targeted with superior firepower? I’m sorry for the tragic losses down there in Gaza, but when you so foolishly crowd around a flaming hunk of metal that contains still-living wanted terrorists, you take your life into your own hands. In this instance, not only the terrorists who were illegally operating out of civilian areas, but also the civilians who acted so irresponsibly are accountable for the harm that came to them. No doubt the world, and in particular the media, will ignore these aspects of today’s events, and focus only on the fact that an Israeli missile killed 11 “Palestinians.” PLO chief Mahmoud Abbas has already given them their headline: > Israel is trying to “wipe out the Palestinian people.” Of course, if that were true, there would be no more “Palestinian people.”
