Freeing Terrorists Is a Bad Idea. Here's Why

Nov 16th, 2006

Prime Minister Ehud Olmert has given his “word of honor” to release hundreds, perhaps thousands of “Palestinian” security prisoners as a goodwill gestures he hopes will move the two sides closer to peace. Why is this a bad idea? Two reasons. First, and most obvious, is that doing so will encourage terrorists to carry out more of the kinds of acts of terror they believe forced Israel to implement the release. Last summer’s abductions of three IDF soldiers by Hamas and Hizb’allah will be held up as what forced Israel to free the jailed “Palestinians,” and a wave of kidnapping will ensue. The second, and more dangerous reason why releasing “Palestinian” terrorists is an act of lunacy has to do with legitimizing the “Palestinian cause” and its violent methods. On the international stage, the “Palestinians” argue that every peace process in modern history has been accompanied by the release of imprisoned enemy combatants. For Israel to free jailed “Palestinian” terrorists would suggest - to the world, but more importantly to the Arabs - that these killers are in fact legitimate combatants and that their actions were legitimate acts of war. Either these people are cold-blooded terrorist killers whose crimes go far beyond legitimate resistance, or they are freedom fighters. Israel cannot have it both ways. Even if Israel made peace with the Arabs based on the creation of a Palestinian Arab state, the terrorist prisoners should never be released because of the things they have done - that is how criminal justice works. If Israel frees these murderers, it will be effectively saying they are not criminals or murderers, but rather legitimate freedom fighters whose actions are excusable in the framework of national resistance. It will be spitting on the blood of its dead, and digging the graves of the next Jewish victims.
